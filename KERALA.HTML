WELCOME TO KERALA!!

Kerala is known for its ecotourism initiatives which include mountaineering, trekking and bird-watching programmes in the Western Ghats as the major activities. The state's tourism industry is a major contributor to the state's economy, growing at the rate of 13.3%.

1.ALLEPPEY (Alappuzha)

There is the whole of Kerala in one side, and then there is this heavenly tourist destination called Alappuzha or Alleppey! Esteemed as the ‘Backwater Capital of India’ or the ‘Venice of the East’, Alleppey is known for its silent backwaters and bountiful beauty! Seated on the banks of the azure Vembanad Lake, it is in fact one of the most popular backwater destinations in Kerala and one of the highest sought-aftertourist places in Kerala. Visit this paradise; enjoy houseboat cruise and stay, village walks, Ayurvedic therapies and lot more!

2. WAYANAD

Cradled in the lap of the Western Ghats, Wayanad truly deserves a top rank in the list of places to visit in Kerala. Situated at an astounding height of 700-2,100m, this stunning hill station is all about fantasy, untouched nature, unforgettable moments, and merriment! Adding more to Wayanad’s enchanting beauty, visitors can also revisit and unfold history while visiting this hilly retreat. Yes, the lush and pristine forests in Wayanad are said to be inhabited more than 3,000 years ago.

3. COCHIN

Cochin is a unique placein God’s Own Country that is perfectly blended with cultural values and modern ideas! Fondly called the ‘Queen of the Arabian Sea’, this imposing port-city has always been an attraction point since the beginning of history. A melting point of numerous culture and traditions, Cochin has embraced all the modern outlook to become one of the most developed metropolis in India

4. MUNNAR

Yet another gorgeous hill station in the lap of the fascinating Western Ghats, Munnar needs no introduction. Rising 1,600m above the sea level, a vacation in the beguiling locales of this hilly retreat is all about the lofty clouds, picturesque mountains, rolling hills, and a soothing ambience. Canopied mostly by never-ending tea plantations and lush forests, you simply cannot miss out Munnar while searching for the best tourist destination in Kerala.

5. KUMARAKOM

Adorned by the magical charm of the Vembanad Lake, Kumarakom is another beguiling backwater destination in God’s Own Country. Located within the proximity to Kottayam city, this is the place where even being lazy is also one of the best things you can do during your Kerala holidays. Adorned by the charm of the emerald Vembanad Lake, the evergreen coconut grooves, breezy rice fields, and lot more, Kumarakom is popular among all the types of visitors.

6. KOVALAM

If you are a beach goer, and is still assuming none can beat the charm and allurement of Goa or other popular beach destinations, you must visit Kovalam today! Located along the coastlines of the charismatic Arabian Sea, this beach town is undoubtedly one of the best tourist attractions to visit in Kerala. Located only 16km from Thiruvananthapuram, the capital of Kerala, Kovalam is the home to some of the most picturesque and playful beaches in the country.

7 top things to do in keralato get the most of your trip
1. Stay in a Houseboat

Kerala is known for its extensive backwaters. They are of huge cultural and economic importance to the state and have been a significant part of Kerala’s tourism industry. With every inch of the state’s coastal area covered with the mystical backwater, a visit to them is a must.

2. Explore the Many Nature Parks of Kerala

Kerala boasts varied wildlife and natural habitats. The lush greenery is perfect for a variety of flora and fauna to make it their home. Eravikulam, Periyar, Chinnar, and Wayanad are some of the best wildlife sanctuaries in Kerala that are worth a visit. You can engage in jungle safaris, overnight stays and camping, photography, and wildlife spotting as you explore the many wildlife habitats Kerala has to offer.

3. Have Tea at the Tea Gardens

South India houses far-stretching lands of tea plantations. Some of the famous ones include the tea plantations of Munnar, Wayanad and Idukki. The experience of sipping a cup of hot Tea with Kerala snacks as you look over the vast fields of tea plants is unmatchable. The aromatic Tea with the aroma of spices wafting around you is something else, making it one of the most incredible things to do in Kerala.

4. Watch the Martial Art of Kalaripayattu

Kalaripayattu is a famous martial art in Kerala that you must have virtually seen in South Indian flicks. Rooted in 2000 B.C., it employs a variety of techniques, including blows, kicks, grappling, pre-established forms, weapons, and even healing techniques. You can witness an hour-long performance of Kalaripayattu at the Kadathanadan Kalari Centre in Thekaddy that showcases more than 27 weapons and forms of this art..

5. Watch Kathakali

We promise you won’t be able to take your eyes off the marvellous dance of Kathakali. The riot of colour with extensive and gracious facial expressions is a treat to the eyes. Kathakali is a dance-drama that tells a story through dance, costumes, and facial expressions – the art of storytelling is strongly portrayed here. In order to acquire the art of Kathakali, performers endure rigorous training that includes hours of eye exercises. See a show at the Kerala Kathakali Centre at the Cochin Cultural Centre.

6. Have an Ayurvedic Massage

Set out on a rejuvenating vacation among Kerala’s entrancing charm, where the age-old practice of Ayurveda unfurls like a fragrant tapestry. Ayurvedic massages have a mystical touch that offers a transformational experience amidst the many things to do in Kerala. Imagine yourself in an oasis of fragrant oils, expert hands guiding you towards calm, and the cares of life dissolving into happiness.

7. Bathe an Elephant at Kodanad Elephant Sanctuary

Imagine giving a bath to elephants, watching baby elephants spray water at each other, and riding the huge elephants of Kerala. The Kodanad Elephant Sanctuary provides all these experiences and more. Kodanad, which is only 42 kilometres from Kochi, provides a thrilling range of activities, like feeding, safari, and elephant bathing. See the allure of young elephants in their environment.
